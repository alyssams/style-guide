Welcome to the Lexus Print Style Guide

Introduction

As we move toward an increasingly global market, these guidelines were created to demonstrate a look and feel for Lexus in regard to design, typography and photography. Together, these elements comprise a single bold statement about the Lexus brand—one that is emotional, distinctive and confident. This new direction is visual evidence of the idea that Lexus engineers create not only remarkable cars but also powerful experiences. Here, the visual look and feel juxtaposes the precision and style of big, bold photographs that carry emotion, glamour and sophistication with the rational and comprehensive information of the type treatments and page design. In attitude and execution, this look draws inspiration from high-end fashion and progressive luxury brands.

Design Key Elements

The following section is intended to demonstrate a variety of structure options, taking into consideration the opportunities and limitations of a given media space. Each structure execution represents common layout types, while looking at variations in typographic treatments and headline length. Each element is created to be modular in order to allow for maximum flexibility.

Typography

The typeface Nobel should be used for all communication collateral—both internal and external. The use of Nobel typography in Regular and Light in All Caps offers a sophisticated and nuanced design that is highly legible and evocative. The font family includes different versions or “weights,” but for consistency, only the examples indicated should be used. As all typefaces are designed for balance and optimum legibility, it is therefore unnecessary to overbold, underline or italicize any of the versions shown opposite. The use of italics is only permitted for titles— (including, but not limited to) newspapers, journals and magazine publications—as well as works of art, emphasis (must be used sparingly), and quote citations.

Tracking

Tracking is the overall spacing of characters within a word, sentence or paragraph. It is an important typographic treatment that ensures visual balance and overall aesthetic form when setting type. Adjusted tracking is required when setting typographic elements throughout all our communications collateral. Correct adjustments to tracking values ensure that each example of typographic usage is balanced and attractive. Shown opposite are examples of typography outlining basic tracking principles. It is important to consider that tracking values will vary depending on size and scale of typography, and for this reason, the principles opposite are for guidance purposes only.

Headlines

Focused on maximizing impact and legibility, headlines should follow the guides set forth on this page. Of note are the variations in execu- tion based on layout type, length of headline, and placement in the overall structure, as well as minimalist tracking and precise kerning to provide a bold, clean look.

Subheads

Subheads are meant to support or pay off the corresponding headline. Like headlines, subheads can either be traditional subhead copy or model nomenclature.

Specs

Specs provide technical information for the vehicle or vehicles featured. They play a minor supporting role in the overall visual of the information structure.

Additional Copy

Any additional copy is optional and should ADDITIONAL be treated with minimal emphasis.

Rules

The rules used within the typographic structure are designed to lead the eye, provide support to the information hierarchy and provide clear space for the other elements within the structure.

Lexus Brand Signature

The permitted versions of the Lexus brand signature should always be used correctly and never altered. Special consideration should be given to placement within the structure, as well as appropriate amounts of clear space to ensure visibility.

Badges

The use of official Lexus badges should be considered a special circumstance and, as such, should not be integrated into every lockup. Placement of badges may vary and will depend on the constraints of the final media. However, it is recommended that the badge be placed over the image area tucked into one of the four available corners.

Social Media

Though the use of social media icons is discouraged for print, there may be project or campaign specific work that requires their use. If deemed appropriate for a given media, the appearance of social icons should be consistent with typographic sizes and colors in order to be effectively integrated into the lockup. Again, their implementation is on a case-by- case basis and should be used sparingly.

Color

The Lexus color palette shown here is the foundation of all Lexus communication and is kept intentionally simple to allow for ease of viewing. Notice the use of a gradient as a background color. This should be used to enhance the media space and add dimension, as well as an element of luxury.

Legal/Cutlines

Within any given layout, all legal copy should have the least prominence while maintaining readability on both light and dark backgrounds.
