Typography

The typeface Nobel should be used for all communication collateral—both internal and external. The use of Nobel typography in Regular and Light in All Caps offers a sophisticated and nuanced design that is highly legible and evocative. The font family includes different versions or “weights,” but for consistency, only the examples indicated should be used. As all typefaces are designed for balance and optimum legibility, it is therefore unnecessary to overbold, underline or italicize any of the versions shown opposite. The use of italics is only permitted for titles— (including, but not limited to) newspapers, journals and magazine publications—as well as works of art, emphasis (must be used sparingly), and quote citations.

Tracking

Tracking is the overall spacing of characters within a word, sentence or paragraph. It is an important typographic treatment that ensures visual balance and overall aesthetic form when setting type.

Adjusted tracking is required when setting typographic elements throughout all our communications collateral. Correct adjustments to tracking values ensure that each example of typographic usage is balanced and attractive.

t is important to consider that tracking values will vary depending on size and scale of typography, and for this reason, the principles opposite are for guidance purposes only.

Leading

Leading [pronounced led-ING] is the variable distance between lines of text within a block of copy.

Headline blocks, primarily when used in advertising or retail application, are large-scale and impactful graphic elements.

Subsequently, they are one of the most important graphic elements on a page. Therefore, when typesetting headlines, attention to detail is paramount.

Kerning

Kerning is the variable distance between characters. It is an important typographic treatment that ensures visual balance and overall aesthetic form when setting type.

Adjusting kerning manually, while not always necessary, is particularly useful when setting large headlines of just two words of similar length. Although instances such as this are rare, the hidden message is that we care about the smallest details.

When working within a layout program, such as Adobe InDesign, it is best to begin with kerning set to either “Metrics” or “Optical” and make the appropriate manual kerning adjustments as needed.
